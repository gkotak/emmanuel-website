Safeguarding

The care of children and vulnerable adults is everyone’s responsibility — and we take it very seriously at Emmanuel.

At Emmanuel we take safeguarding and the care of children and vulnerable people very seriously.

Safeguarding is the responsibility of the whole church community. If you have any concern about the safety or wellbeing of a child or adult, please do not hesitate to speak to our Parish Safeguarding Officer or a member of the clergy.

Parish Safeguarding Officer

Sarah Watts

For any safeguarding concern or question, Sarah can be reached directly by email.

safeguarding@emmanuelnw6.com

Link to the parish safeguarding policy to follow.

In an emergency

If someone is in immediate danger

If a child or adult is in immediate danger, always call the emergency services on 999. You can also contact the Diocese of London safeguarding team, or the NSPCC and other national helplines, for confidential advice at any time.

Diocesan safeguarding contact details and national helpline numbers to be confirmed with the Parish Safeguarding Officer.

Everyone is responsible.

If something doesn't feel right, please speak up. Our Parish Safeguarding Officer is here to listen and to help.
